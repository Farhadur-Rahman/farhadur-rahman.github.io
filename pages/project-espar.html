ESPAR
Climate Resilient Agro-farming

Funder: Asia Pacific Network  |  Status: Completed

Full Title
Enabling Stakeholder Participation and Applied Research in Policy Comprehension to Mainstream Climate Resilient Agro-farming Practices in National Climate Agenda
Funder
Asia Pacific Network for Global Change Research (APN)
Duration
2023 – 2024
Role
Co-Principal Investigator

Overview

ESPAR aimed to mainstream climate-resilient agro-farming practices into Bangladesh's national climate policy agenda by building the evidence base and stakeholder engagement needed for policy uptake. The project combined applied geospatial research with participatory stakeholder workshops to bridge the gap between scientific evidence and policy implementation.

Activities

Dr. Rahman led the remote sensing and spatial analysis component, mapping agro-ecosystem vulnerability to climate extremes (flood, drought, salinity intrusion) at district scale. Results were integrated with stakeholder perception surveys to identify priority areas for climate-smart agriculture interventions.

ESPAR also contributed to the CLARE Capacity Strengthening Hub, supporting training workshops in Maldives and the Philippines on climate adaptation and resilience methodologies (2023–2024).

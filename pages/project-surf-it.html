Full Title
Spatial Surge Forecasting Using Artificial Intelligence and Community Knowledge for Inclusive and Transformative Early Actions
Funder
IDRC — International Development Research Centre, Canada
Duration
2024 – 2026
Role
Co-Principal Investigator

Overview

SURF-IT develops an AI-powered flood surge forecasting system that integrates satellite-derived geospatial data with community indigenous knowledge to enable early warning and inclusive response to extreme flood events in Bangladesh.

Bangladesh is among the world's most flood-vulnerable nations. Storm surges from tropical cyclones regularly inundate coastal and riverine areas, displacing millions. Traditional early warning systems often fail to reach the most vulnerable communities in time, and rely on limited data inputs.

Approach

SURF-IT combines machine learning flood models trained on historical satellite flood inundation data, topographic LiDAR, and river discharge records with participatory mapping of community flood experience and local adaptation strategies. The result is a spatially explicit, community-validated surge forecasting tool with actionable early warning outputs.

Dr. Rahman leads the geospatial analysis component — applying remote sensing data to characterise flood exposure, map vulnerable populations, and validate model predictions against field observations.

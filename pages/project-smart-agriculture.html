Overview

This research programme applies UAV (Unmanned Aerial Vehicle) remote sensing to transform crop monitoring and yield estimation in Bangladesh's agricultural systems. Using both RGB and multispectral cameras mounted on drones, the team captures high-resolution imagery of field crops at key phenological stages.

The programme addresses two critical challenges in Bangladesh agriculture: (1) the need for precision crop management to optimise inputs and reduce waste, and (2) early detection of stress (drought, heat, nutrient deficiency) to enable timely interventions.

Selected Studies

Wheat yield under climate stress — UAV-derived vegetation indices (NDVI, NDRE, LAI) measured at multiple growth stages were used to build predictive models for heat and drought-induced yield loss in wheat varieties. Published in Smart Agricultural Technology (2025).

Okra and nitrogen fertilisation — Multispectral UAV surveys optimised nitrogen application rates across okra variety trials, demonstrating 30% input savings with maintained yield. Published in Smart Agricultural Technology (2025).

Soybean water-deficit stress — Integrated UAV remote sensing with field trials to evaluate seaweed extract as a drought mitigation strategy. Published in Drones (2025).

"A forest ecologist who applies the most advanced remote sensing techniques to understand and protect forest ecosystems."

Dr. Md Farhadur Rahman is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Remote Sensing and GIS at Gazipur Agricultural University (GAU) in Bangladesh. His academic journey reflects both determination and vision — bridging cutting-edge geospatial technologies with the ecological realities of forests.

Supported by the Japanese Government (MONBUKAGAKUSHO: MEXT), he joined Kyoto University in 2016, completing his Ph.D. in March 2022. His doctoral work covered 230 km² of forest landscapes in central Kyoto using airborne LiDAR, distilling ecologically meaningful insights from massive datasets.

After completing his Ph.D., Dr. Rahman contributed to The Digital Biosphere Project at Kyoto University — an innovative initiative integrating genomes, traits, and ecology to maximize forest function. At GAU, he is expanding into SMART agriculture, applying geospatial technologies to enhance resilience and sustainability in agro-ecosystems.

Research Interests

◈

LiDAR Applications in Forest Ecology

Pioneering use of airborne LiDAR to analyze forest structure and disturbance dynamics. High-resolution data transforms ecological monitoring for precision forestry.

Airborne LiDAR Canopy Height Forest Structure
⚡

Forest Disaster Dynamics

Investigating how typhoons, storms, and liana infestations alter forest structure, biomass, and recovery. Combining LiDAR, satellite imagery, and ecological modelling.

Typhoons Liana Infestation Biomass Resilience
◎

Land Use Change & Modeling

Applying remote sensing and GIS to track land use changes, fragmentation, and degradation. Predictive models explore how landscapes evolve under climate change.

GIS Fragmentation Cellular Automata ANN
❋

Plant Physiological Ecology

Studying leaf spectral reflectance and photosynthesis in Quercus species, linking physiological traits to broader ecological processes at ecosystem scale.

Spectral Reflectance Photosynthesis Quercus
⊕

Landscape Ecology & Restoration

Examining spatial patterns to design restoration strategies for degraded ecosystems. Integrating local knowledge with scientific tools for inclusive restoration.

Restoration Community-based Spatial Patterns
✦

SMART Agriculture

Expanding into geospatial technologies for agro-ecosystems — UAV remote sensing for crop monitoring, yield prediction, and sustainable farming under climate stress.
